Types and efficacy of Herbal Teas

Chamomile

Chamomile is one of the most popular and easy to drink herbal teas with a delicious sweet apple-like aroma. It is considered a relaxing herb with anti-inflammatory, soothing, cooling, and digestive properties. Drinking chamomile tea before going to sleep can help improve the quality of sleep and insomnia. The steam from chamomile tea is also effective for hay fever and nasal congestion.

Lavender

Lavender is a popular herb that is famous for its ability to soothe and relax anxiety, nervousness and irritability. Lavender is a popular herb known for its ability to relax and calm anxiety and nervousness. It helps relieve tension and helps you fall into a deep sleep.

Rosehips

Rosehips contain abundant vitamin C, more than 20 times as much as lemons! Rosehip also contains iron and minerals, and is caffeine free, so it can be consumed safely during pregnancy to supplement nutrients that are often lacking. It contains iron and minerals and is caffeine free, so it can be drunk during pregnancy.

Lemon grass

Lemongrass has long been used as a medicine for fevers and other ailments. It is still an indispensable herb in Vietnamese and Thai cuisine. Lemongrass has many beneficial effects such as reducing swelling, sterilizing, relieving upset stomach, and relieving pain and fever. It is a delicious herb tea popular for its refreshing lemon-like aroma, and since it has no particular taste, it is ideal for blending with other herbs. Dandelion

Dandelion, also known as Dandelion Coffee, is a caffeine-free tea made from the root of the dandelion plant. It is caffeine free and can be consumed during pregnancy without concern. It is effective in balancing hormones, improving stomach and liver functions, and relieving cold and constipation.。
